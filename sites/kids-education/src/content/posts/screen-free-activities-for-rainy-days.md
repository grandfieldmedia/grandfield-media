Rainy days don't have to mean screen time all day. Here are ten easy, screen-free
activities that keep kids engaged — most need only paper, crayons, and a few
things you already have at home.

## 1. Coloring pages

The classic for a reason. Themed coloring pages (animals, seasons, letters) keep
little hands busy and build fine-motor skills.

## 2. Dot-to-dot puzzles

Great for number practice disguised as fun — kids connect the dots to reveal a
picture.

## 3. Simple mazes

Mazes build focus and problem-solving. Start easy and work up as they get confident.

## 4. Cut-and-paste sorting

Print pictures to cut out and sort by color, shape, or category — scissors skills
plus early logic.

## 5. Letter and number tracing

Tracing sheets are the gentlest way to build early writing confidence.

## 6–10. Mix in the classics

Build a pillow fort, do a scavenger hunt around the house, bake something simple
together, play "I spy," or put on a puppet show. The goal isn't perfection — it's
keeping curious minds busy.

> Adult supervision is always recommended for young children.
