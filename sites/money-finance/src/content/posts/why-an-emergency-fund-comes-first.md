If you only build one financial habit this year, make it a small emergency fund.
Here's why it beats almost everything else on the priority list.

## It stops the debt spiral

Without a buffer, every surprise — a car repair, a medical bill, a broken phone —
goes on a credit card. The emergency fund breaks that cycle by turning
emergencies into inconveniences.

## You don't need a fortune to start

The classic advice is "three to six months of expenses," and that's a great
long-term target. But the first milestone is much smaller: **one starter amount**
that covers a typical unexpected bill. For many people that's enough to change
how safe their finances feel.

## Where to keep it

Somewhere separate from your everyday spending, but still easy to reach — a
plain savings account works. The point is that it's *there* and it's *not*
tempting to dip into.

## Build it automatically

Set a small recurring transfer the day after payday. Automating it means you
never have to rely on willpower, and the balance grows in the background.
